Privacy Policy

Last updated: 16 June 2026

1. Who We Are (Data Controller)

Open Savanna Limited (Company No. PVT-271J5W2D), a private company registered in Kenya ("we", "our", or "us"), is the data controller responsible for your personal data. This policy explains how we collect, use, store, share, and protect your personal data, and your rights, in line with the Data Protection Act, 2019 of Kenya and the regulations made under it.

  • Registered entity: Open Savanna Limited (PVT-271J5W2D)
  • Location: Nairobi, Kenya
  • Privacy contact: official@opensavanna.com · +254 735 912 322

2. Personal Data We Collect

Information you provide

  • Contact information: name, email address, phone number (e.g. when you book a call or message us)
  • Business information: company name, role, and project details you share during consultations
  • Payment information: billing details processed through third-party providers (M-Pesa, bank transfer). We do not store full card numbers or M-Pesa PINs.

Information collected automatically

  • Usage data: pages visited, time on site, referring website (via Google Analytics — only if you consent; see section 7)
  • Device data: browser type, operating system, and IP-derived approximate location

3. Why We Use Your Data, and Our Lawful Basis

Under section 30 of the Data Protection Act, 2019, we process your personal data on the following bases:

  • To respond to enquiries and provide our services — performance of a contract / steps prior to a contract
  • To process payments and keep financial records — legal obligation and contract
  • To send service updates or marketing — your consent (which you may withdraw at any time)
  • To understand and improve our website (analytics) — your consent
  • To protect our rights and comply with the law — legal obligation / legitimate interests

4. Who We Share It With

We do not sell your personal data. We share it only with:

  • Service providers who help us operate: Cal.com (scheduling), Google (Workspace email and Google Analytics), Vercel (website hosting), and payment processors
  • Authorities where required by law or to establish, exercise, or defend legal claims
  • Project partners or sub-contractors — only where necessary to deliver a project to you, and under confidentiality and data-protection obligations

5. International Data Transfers

Some of our providers (e.g. Google and Vercel) store and process data on servers outside Kenya. Where your personal data is transferred outside Kenya, we rely on appropriate safeguards (such as the providers' contractual data-protection commitments) and/or your consent, as required by sections 48–49 of the Data Protection Act, 2019.

6. How Long We Keep It

  • Enquiry / contact data: up to 24 months after our last interaction, unless you become a client
  • Client and financial records: as long as required by Kenyan tax and company law (generally up to 7 years)
  • Analytics data: in line with our Google Analytics retention settings

We delete or anonymise data once it is no longer needed.

7. Cookies & Analytics

Our site uses a small number of cookies for basic functionality and, only with your consent, Google Analytics 4 to understand how the site is used. When you first visit, a banner lets you Accept or Reject analytics; we do not load Google Analytics until you accept, and you can change your choice by clearing your browser's stored site data for this site. We do not use cookies for third-party advertising.

8. Your Rights

Under section 26 of the Data Protection Act, 2019, you have the right to:

  • Be informed of how your data is used
  • Access the personal data we hold about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Request deletion of your data
  • Object to or restrict processing, and withdraw consent at any time

To exercise any of these rights, email official@opensavanna.com. We will respond within the timelines required by law.

9. Data Security & Breaches

We apply appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your data. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, but in the event of a personal-data breach that poses a risk to your rights, we will notify the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner within 72 hours and affected individuals where required.

10. Third-Party Links

Our website links to third-party sites (e.g. LinkedIn, WhatsApp, Cal.com). We are not responsible for their privacy practices; please review their own policies.

11. Complaints

If you have concerns about how we handle your data, please contact us first at official@opensavanna.com. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC), Kenya — www.odpc.go.ke.

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be posted here with a revised date.
